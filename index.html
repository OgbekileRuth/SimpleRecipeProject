OHA SOUP

Soup mostly eaten by the igbo people in the southern part of Nigeria. It is commonly prepared from an evergreen tree whose botanical name is Pterocarpus mildraedii

Oha Ingredients

  • Vegetables - Oha leaves
  • Cocoyam - 8 small corms
  • Red palm oil - 3 cooking spoon
  • Assorted Beef
  • Assorted fish
  • Chill pepper, salt and crayfish(to taste)
  • 2 stock cubes and 1 teaspoon Ogiri Igbo

Step-by-Step Instruction

  1. Grind the crayfish and pepper and set aside
  2. Wash and boil the cocoyam corms till soft remove the peels and pound the corms to a smooth paste
  3. Using your fingers, cut the oha leaves into pieces

Cooking Directions

  • Boil the shaki, stockfish and dryfish
  • Wash the beef add to the pot, add 2 stock cube and continue cooking for 10 minutes
  • Add the pepper, ogiri igbo and ground crayfish and cook for 10 minutes
  • Add the cocoyam lump paste in small lumps
  • Add palm oil, cover the pot and leave to cook on high heat
  • Add the oha leaves and leave to cook for about 5 minutes
  • Add salt to taste and thee soup is ready to served with Eba,Fufu,Amala or Pounded yam

Description Of The Oha Soup

Oha soup is special because the tender oha leaves used in preparing this soup recipe are seasonal. It is a traditional soup similar to the bitterleaf soup but cooked with the oha leaves.
